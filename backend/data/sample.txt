Transformers are a type of neural network architecture used in natural language processing.
They were introduced in the paper "Attention is All You Need".
Transformers rely heavily on the attention mechanism.
Attention allows the model to focus on important parts of the input.
Before transformers, recurrent neural networks were common.
Recurrent networks process tokens sequentially.
Transformers process tokens in parallel.
This makes transformers faster during training.
Transformers also scale better with large datasets.
Large language models are usually based on transformers.
Examples include GPT, BERT, and T5.
